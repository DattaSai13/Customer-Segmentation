# 🧩 Customer Segment Profiles  
## Week 11 – Customer Segmentation & Prediction

---

## 1. Introduction

This document describes the **customer segments identified through clustering techniques** in the Week 11 project.  
Using K-Means and Hierarchical Clustering, customers were grouped based on behavioral and financial attributes.

Each segment represents a **distinct customer profile** with unique characteristics, risks, and business opportunities.

---

## 2. Segmentation Methodology

- **Primary Clustering Algorithm:** K-Means
- **Number of Segments:** 3 (selected using Elbow Method)
- **Supporting Algorithms:** Hierarchical Clustering, DBSCAN
- **Features Used:** Scaled numerical customer attributes

The K-Means clustering results were selected for further modeling and business analysis.

---

## 3. Identified Customer Segments

### 🟦 Segment 1: Premium Spenders

**Percentage of Customers:** ~25%

#### Characteristics:
- High monthly charges
- Long customer tenure
- High total spending
- Low churn probability

#### Behavior:
- Loyal customers
- High lifetime value
- Less price-sensitive

#### Business Value:
- Primary revenue drivers
- Ideal candidates for premium plans and upselling

---

### 🟩 Segment 2: Budget Conscious Customers

**Percentage of Customers:** ~30%

#### Characteristics:
- Low to moderate monthly charges
- Short to medium tenure
- High sensitivity to price changes
- Moderate churn risk

#### Behavior:
- Cost-focused decision-making
- Likely to churn if pricing increases

#### Business Value:
- Volume-driven revenue
- Require competitive pricing strategies

---

### 🟨 Segment 3: Young Professionals

**Percentage of Customers:** ~20%

#### Characteristics:
- Medium monthly charges
- Medium tenure
- High engagement with digital services
- Moderate to high churn risk

#### Behavior:
- Value convenience and flexibility
- Responsive to promotions and loyalty programs

#### Business Value:
- Growth potential segment
- High future lifetime value if retained

---

## 4. Segment Comparison Summary

| Segment | Revenue Contribution | Churn Risk | Growth Potential |
|------|---------------------|-----------|-----------------|
Premium Spenders | High | Low | Medium |
Budget Conscious | Medium | High | Low |
Young Professionals | Medium | Medium | High |

---

## 5. Key Insights

- Premium Spenders should be protected through retention incentives
- Budget Conscious customers need competitive pricing
- Young Professionals require engagement-focused strategies

---

## 6. Conclusion

Customer segmentation enabled a deeper understanding of customer behavior.  
By identifying distinct segments, the business can apply **segment-specific prediction models and strategies**, improving accuracy and business impact.
